\section{VII.A Hilbert Space of Identical Particles}

In chapter IV, we discussed the Gibbs paradox for the mixing entropy of gases of identical particles. This difficulty was overcome by postulating that the phase space for NNN identical particles must be divided by N!N !N!, the number of permutations. This is not quite satisfactory as the classical equations of motion implicitly treat the particles as distinct. In quantum mechanics, by contrast, the identity of particles appears at the level of allowed states in Hilbert space. For example, the probability of finding two identical particles at positions x⃗1\vec{x}_{1}x1​ and x⃗2\vec{x}_{2}x2​ is ∣Ψ(x⃗1,x⃗2)∣2.\left|\Psi\left(\vec{x}_{1}, \vec{x}_{2}\right)\right|^{2} .∣Ψ(x1​,x2​)∣2. Since the exchange of particles 1 and 2 leads to the same configurations, we must have ∣Ψ(x⃗1,x⃗2)∣2=∣Ψ(x⃗2,x⃗1)∣2\left|\Psi\left(\vec{x}_{1}, \vec{x}_{2}\right)\right|^{2}=\left|\Psi\left(\vec{x}_{2}, \vec{x}_{1}\right)\right|^{2}∣Ψ(x1​,x2​)∣2=∣Ψ(x2​,x1​)∣2.
